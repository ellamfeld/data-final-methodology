Methodology for "Despite Parents Sounding the Alarm, Chronic School Bus Delays Persist Across New York City"

By Ella Feldman

MAY 20, 2025

For my final data project, I reported on chronic school bus delays in New York City. You can read my final story here.

To complete my data analysis, I used the Bus Breakdown and Delays dataset from the Department of Education, published on NYC OpenData.

The dataset includes every school year from 2015-2016 through now. Each row represents one school bus delay or breakdown. To calculate the total number of delays and breakdowns per school year, I calculated the number of rows each school year had. I excluded the 2024-2025 school year in my value count because it is still ongoing.

In order to compare this school year to previous ones, I decided to calculate the daily average for the last four school years, including 2024-2025 through May 19, 2025. To do this, I took the total number of delays per school year and divided it by the total number of school days in that school year so far.

After making that comparison, I focused my calculations on the 2024-2025 school year through May 19, 2025. For my daily average per month chart, I stopped at April, since May is ongoing.

This data analysis will be better after June 26, 2025, when all the data for the 2024-2025 school year has been collected. I plan to re-analyze the numbers then. Still, by using the average number of delays per day as my primary metric, I believe my data analysis is as accurate and fair as it can be today.
